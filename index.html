The Cashion Bread Favor Recipe

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, dump the mason jar contents (flour, salt, yeast) and combine with 3/4 cups room temp water until the mixture is very sticky and no lumps of flour remain.
  2. Cover the bowl and place in a warm, draft-free area until doubled in size, about 1.5 to 2 hours.
  3. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees. Place a large empty Dutch oven with a lid in the oven for at least 30 minutes (if you do not have one, you can preheat a baking sheet instead). Next, uncover your risen dough and using lightly floured hands, reach down one side of the dough to the bottom of the bowl, pull up, and stretch the edge of the dough over the center toward the opposite side. Turn the bowl 90 degrees and repeat the folding and turning, working in a circle around the dough until it starts to hold its shape, about 8 to 10 rotations. Turn out the dough onto a lightly floured surface, placing it seam side down. Shape into a round loaf.
  4. Transfer the dough onto parchment paper and sprinkle it with flour. Loosely cover with plastic wrap or a towel for 10 minutes. Using a sharp knife or bread lame, cut a slit about 1/4 inch deep across the top of the dough.
  5. Place the dough with the parchment paper directly into the preheated Dutch oven. Cover with the lid. Bake for 20-25 minutes. Remove the lid and bake until deeply golden brown, about 15 to 17 minutes more. If you are using a baking sheet, place the dough with parchment paper onto the baking sheet and bake anywhere from 30-40 minutes, or until your desired color.
  6. Carefully remove the artisan bread, and place it on a wire rack/counter to cool for at least 20 minutes before slicing.
